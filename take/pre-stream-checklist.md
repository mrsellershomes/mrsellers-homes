# Pre-Stream Checklist — The Sellers Take

> The whole point: tell the truth about the Bergen County market, no BS, with the analysis you'd give your own family. The market doesn't care about anyone's feelings. Your job is to read it honestly.

---

## Voice reminder — read this every time before going live

- "That was a good buy." / "That was a weird buy." / "I wouldn't have done that for myself."
- "No hate — everybody's different."
- The market doesn't care about feelings. Yours, the buyers', the sellers', or mine.
- When buyers complain sellers are "greedy" or houses are "overpriced" — the market closed at that price. That's the truth. Their feelings are not a counterargument.
- You're not selling yourself. You're educating. Clients will come because of how you think, not because you asked for them.

---

## 30 min before — content prep

### 1. Recent solds (the workhorse segment)

- [ ] Pull Bergen County MLS hot sheet → **recently sold, last 7 days**
- [ ] Pick **2–4 listings**:
  - 1–2 you think were **solid buys**
  - 1–2 you think were **weird / overpriced / "I wouldn't have done it"**
- [ ] For each, note: **town, list price, sold price, days on market, # of offers** (if you know)
- [ ] Have your honest take ready before going live (one sentence each)

### 2. Housing Wire (your subscriber edge)

- [ ] Skim Housing Wire homepage — past 7 days
- [ ] Pick **1 gated article** the public can't read
- [ ] Your angle: does it apply to Bergen, or is it a national story that breaks down at the local level? *That's the segment.*

### 3. Local color — pick 1–2

- [ ] **northjersey.com** — anything Bergen-specific worth reacting to?
- [ ] **Boozy Burbs** — new restaurant / lifestyle / "Bergen is changing" angle
- [ ] **201 Magazine** — local cultural context
- [ ] **Real Estate Twitter** — any take this week that needs a Bergen reality check?

### 4. Buyer-mindset segment (always include)

Your show's spine. At least one segment per stream should hit the same theme:
**buyers complaining about prices is not a market signal — closed sales are.**

Possible angles:
- "Here's what buyers got wrong this week"
- "The 5-offer house and the buyer who walked away"
- "Why 'shame the sellers' is a losing game plan"
- "What the market is telling you when you ignore your feelings"

### 5. Build the docket (5 min)

- [ ] Open **https://mrsellers.homes/take/control**
- [ ] Type **4–6 topics** in the docket textarea (one per line, each under 70 chars):
  - Lead with your sharpest take
  - Mix: data + opinion + reaction + local
- [ ] **Hot Take quote** — your single best line for the day (used for the F5 pull-quote scene + Shorts clips later)
- [ ] **Next Episode** — when's the next one? (or leave blank for "TURN ON NOTIFICATIONS")
- [ ] **Start in (minutes)** — usually 5–10 (gives YouTube time to spool up)
- [ ] Hit **SAVE DOCKET**

## 5 min before — tech check

- [ ] OBS open → Scene Collection: **"The Sellers Take"**
- [ ] Speak into the mic — **Audio Mixer bar bouncing -18 to -12 dB** (loud peaks at -6, never red)
- [ ] Webcam preview: framing right, light on, you look like a human
- [ ] Speed test: **upload > 5 Mbps** (anything less and YouTube will choke)
- [ ] YouTube Studio open → Create → Go Live → stream key linked or signed in
- [ ] Phone on **Do Not Disturb**
- [ ] Water nearby
- [ ] Email/Slack/notifications muted

---

## Go time

- [ ] OBS → **Start Streaming** (lands on Starting Soon scene — countdown ticking)
- [ ] When ready to actually start: **F2** (Intro animation, 6 sec) → **F3** (Main)
- [ ] In control panel: **click your first topic** → ticker on stream updates to "TOPIC 1/N"

---

## During the stream — segment flow

For every topic:

1. **Click it** in the control panel → ticker on stream updates → audience sees the new segment
2. **Talk it through** — 5–10 min per topic typically
3. **Next Topic →** when you wrap that segment
4. **F5 (Hot Take card)** when you land a punchy line worth a Shorts clip — leave it up 8–10 sec, then **F3** back to Main
5. **F4 (Screen Share)** when you want to show MLS solds, a Housing Wire article, a Twitter post — switch the actual on-screen window first, then hit F4

If you finish your docket early: keep going on whatever's hot from chat, or add another topic on the fly via the control panel.

---

## Wrap

- [ ] **F8** (Ending scene) — hold ~15 sec to let viewers absorb the next-episode time + handles
- [ ] OBS → **Stop Streaming**
- [ ] YouTube Studio → **End Stream** (if it doesn't auto-end)
- [ ] **Download the recording** while it's fresh — that's your clip source for Shorts/Reels
- [ ] Back on control panel → **CLEAR DOCKET** (resets for next stream)

---

## After the stream — content multiplier

The live stream is the *source*. Real distribution comes from clipping:

- [ ] Pick 2–3 sharp moments from the recording
- [ ] Cut into 30–60 sec vertical clips (hot takes, controversial moments, "good buy / weird buy" calls)
- [ ] Post to: YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels (@tsellnj), TikTok (@tsellnj)
- [ ] Caption hook: lead with the take, not "here's what happened"
- [ ] These clips drive search traffic back to the next live stream + drive client inquiries

That's the actual flywheel. The live stream is upstream of everything.
