# Pre-Stream Checklist — The Sellers Take

> The whole point: tell the truth about the Bergen County market, no BS, with the analysis you'd give your own family. The market doesn't care about anyone's feelings. Your job is to read it honestly.

---

## Voice reminder — read this every time before going live

- "That was a good buy." / "That was a weird buy." / "I wouldn't have done that for myself."
- "No hate — everybody's different."
- The market doesn't care about feelings. Yours, the buyers', the sellers', or mine.
- When buyers complain sellers are "greedy" or houses are "overpriced" — the market closed at that price. That's the truth. Their feelings are not a counterargument.
- You're not selling yourself. You're educating. Clients will come because of how you think, not because you asked for them.

## 30 min before — content prep

### 1. Recent solds (the workhorse segment)

- [ ] Pull Bergen County MLS hot sheet → **recently sold, last 7 days**
- [ ] Pick **2–4 listings**:
  - 1–2 you think were **solid buys**
  - 1–2 you think were **weird / overpriced / "I wouldn't have done it"**
- [ ] For each, note: **town, list price, sold price, days on market, # of offers** (if you know)
- [ ] Have your honest take ready before going live (one sentence each)

### 2. Housing Wire (your subscriber edge)

- [ ] Skim Housing Wire homepage — past 7 days
- [ ] Pick **1 gated article** the public can't read
- [ ] Your angle: does it apply to Bergen, or is it a national story that breaks down at the local level? *That's the segment.*

### 3. Local color — pick 1–2

- [ ] **northjersey.com** — anything Bergen-specific worth reacting to?
- [ ] **Boozy Burbs** — new restaurant / lifestyle / "Bergen is changing" angle
- [ ] **201 Magazine** — local cultural context
- [ ] **Real Estate Twitter** — any take this week that needs a Bergen reality check?

### 4. Buyer-mindset segment (always include)

Your show's spine. At least one segment per stream should hit the same theme:
**buyers complaining about prices is not a market signal — closed sales are.**

Possible angles:
- "Here's what buyers got wrong this week"
- "The 5-offer house and the buyer who walked away"
- "Why 'shame the sellers' is a losing game plan"
- "What the market is telling you when you ignore your feelings"

### 5. Build the docket (5 min)

- [ ] Open **https://mrsellers.homes/take/control**
- [ ] Type **4–6 topics** in the docket textarea (one per line, each under 70 chars):
  - Lead with your sharpest take
  - Mix: data + opinion + reaction + local
- [ ] **Hot Take quote** — your single best line for the day (used for the F5 pull-quote scene + Shorts clips later)
- [ ] **Next Episode** — when's the next one? (or leave blank for "TURN ON NOTIFICATIONS")
- [ ] **Start in (minutes)** — usually 5–10 (gives YouTube time to spool up)
- [ ] Hit **SAVE DOCKET**

## During the stream — segment flow

For every topic:

1. **Click it** in the control panel → ticker on stream updates → audience sees the new segment
2. **Talk it through** — 5–10 min per topic typically
3. **Next Topic →** when you wrap that segment
4. **F5 (Hot Take card)** when you land a punchy line worth a Shorts clip — leave it up 8–10 sec, then **F3** back to Main
5. **F4 (Screen Share)** when you want to show MLS solds, a Housing Wire article, a Twitter post — switch the actual on-screen window first, then hit F4

If you finish your docket early: keep going on whatever's hot from chat, or add another topic on the fly via the control panel.

## Wrap

- [ ] **F8** (Ending scene) — hold ~15 sec to let viewers absorb the next-episode time + handles
- [ ] OBS → **Stop Streaming**
- [ ] YouTube Studio → **End Stream** (if it doesn't auto-end)
- [ ] **Download the recording** while it's fresh — that's your clip source for Shorts/Reels
- [ ] Back on control panel → **CLEAR DOCKET** (resets for next stream)

## After the stream — content multiplier

The live stream is the *source*. Real distribution comes from clipping:

- [ ] Pick 2–3 sharp moments from the recording
- [ ] Cut into 30–60 sec vertical clips (hot takes, controversial moments, "good buy / weird buy" calls)
- [ ] Post to: YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels (@tsellnj), TikTok (@tsellnj)
- [ ] Caption hook: lead with the take, not "here's what happened"
- [ ] These clips drive search traffic back to the next live stream + drive client inquiries

That's the actual flywheel. The live stream is upstream of everything.

## Beginner essentials — the things that will save you a year of mistakes

Read these once. Re-read after your first 5 streams.

### Delivery

**1. The first 10 seconds decide everything.** People decide whether to stay within 10 seconds of arriving. Do NOT open with "hey guys, welcome to the stream." Open with your sharpest take of the day. *"A house in Ridgewood just sold for $250k over ask. Eleven buyers got destroyed trying to win it. Here's why they all played it wrong — and what most people in this market still don't get."* That's a hook. "Welcome back" is a yawn.

**2. Look at the camera, not your screen.** Most beginners stare at OBS, chat, or themselves. The difference between "feels like a conversation" and "feels like a webinar" is eye contact with the lens. Trick: **stick a Post-it next to the camera with an arrow pointing at the lens.** Glance at chat between segments only.

**3. Talk to ONE person.** Not "you guys." Not "everyone watching." One person, alone with you. *"If you're sitting there thinking the market is rigged…"* hits harder than *"a lot of you are probably thinking…"*

**4. Embrace silence. Pauses are powerful.** Beginners fill every second with "um," "like," "you know." A 2-second pause before a key take makes the take land 3x harder. Listen to any podcast host you respect — they pause. Practice this.

**5. Don't apologize.** "Sorry I'm late," "sorry the audio's weird," "sorry for the technical difficulty." 80% of what you notice, viewers don't. Apologizing draws attention to it. Just keep going.

**6. End strong.** Last thing you say is what people remember. Don't fade out with "alright, that's the show." End with: a take, a CTA (subscribe / notifications), and a specific next-time hook. *"Tomorrow I'm pulling up three sold listings in Glen Rock and telling you which one I would've walked away from. Hit subscribe. See you at 3."*

### Body & energy

**7. Sit forward. Don't slouch.** Energy on stream is half posture. Lean toward the camera. Use your hands. Be in the conversation, not behind it.

**8. Warm up before going live.** Talk out loud for 2–3 minutes before hitting Start Streaming. Sing along to a song. Read a paragraph aloud. Anything that gets your voice out of "internal monologue" mode. You'll sound 30% more present in the first 5 minutes.

**9. Check yourself before going live.** Look at the OBS preview for 10 seconds. Hair? Shirt? Background clutter? Light hitting you from the front (not behind)? Sounds dumb. Matters.

### Mic specifics (your FiFine + UM2)

**10. Mic position matters more than mic quality.** Be **4–6 inches from the mic, off-axis** (mic pointed slightly past your face, not straight at your mouth). This kills plosives ("p" pops) and gives you presence. Beginners think "louder mic = better," then back away from it and sound distant.

**11. Speak AT the mic, not toward it.** When you turn your head sideways to look at a second monitor, your voice drops. If you must move, raise your volume. Better: keep your head pointed at the camera.

### Mindset

**12. No one is watching live at first. That's fine.** Streams 1–30 will have 0–5 live viewers. Normal. The clips you cut afterwards are where the actual reach happens. **Stream like 1000 people are watching, even when 0 are.** Your future audience will discover ep. 1 only after you've made ep. 47.

**13. Consistency beats perfection.** Daily at 70% quality crushes weekly at 90% quality. Algorithm rewards rhythm. So does habit. Don't skip a day because "I'm not feeling it" — that's the day your one viewer becomes a regular.

**14. The "first 1000 streams" rule.** Most quitters bail at ep. 15–25 because growth is flat. Real traction usually starts ep. 50–100 — *if* you've improved each time. The 3M views you got before came from showing up. Same playbook applies here.

**15. Watch your own streams back.** Painful. Necessary. You'll catch verbal tics ("you know" creeping back in), weak openings, segments that dragged. Watch the first 5 streams in full. After that, just review the openings and closings. Your delivery will jump a level in 4 weeks.

### Editorial — for your show specifically

**16. Anchor every claim with a Bergen example.** "The market is hot" = lazy. "147 Cedar in Ridgewood, listed at $995k, sold for $1.285M with 11 offers in 17 days" = authority. Specifics destroy generic. Specifics also help anyone Googling those addresses find your stream.

**17. Bluntness without anger = authority. Bluntness with frustration = crank.** Your "no BS" voice works because it's calm. The moment you start ranting at greedy buyers or whining about NAR or yelling at the camera, you become a guy with a podcast. Stay analytical even when you're disagreeing hard.

**18. "I don't know" is a power move.** When a viewer asks something you don't know, say "I don't know — I'd want to look at the data." That's authority. Faking it is how you lose your audience for good.

**19. Don't promise what you won't deliver.** "Next week I'll do a deep dive on Tenafly" — either do it or never say it. One broken promise erases ten kept ones.

**20. End every stream with the same closer.** Pick a signature sign-off and use it every time. It becomes a brand cue. Examples: "That's the take. See you tomorrow." / "No BS. Same time tomorrow." / "Class dismissed." Whatever fits — but pick one and stick with it for 100 episodes minimum.

### What to do RIGHT after stream 1

- [ ] Watch the whole thing back. Take notes on what bugged you.
- [ ] Cut **one** 30-60 sec vertical clip of the strongest moment. Post to all 3 platforms (Shorts, Reels, TikTok).
- [ ] Don't change anything else yet. Stream again tomorrow.
- [ ] After stream 5, change the **one thing** that bugs you most.
- [ ] Repeat.

That's the loop. There's no shortcut.
